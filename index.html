Short Stories

The old woman closed her eyes, her wrinkled hands clasped around a single dandelion seed. With a sigh, she blew. The seed drifted on the wind, a tiny wish carried aloft.

Little Anika's laughter danced on the wind like the red kite tugging at the string in her hand. Higher and higher it climbed, a speck against the endless blue, until the string went limp. Tears welled in Anika's eyes, but a tiny smile peeked through. For a moment, her kite had kissed the sky.

Moonlight dripped through the attic window, illuminating a forgotten teacup. A spider tiptoed across the rim, then spun a silken bridge to a plump raisin, claiming its midnight feast.
